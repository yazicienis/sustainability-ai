Quantum Computers and AI

Exploring Quantum Computing and Its Impact on Artificial Intelligence

About the Research Group

The Quantum Computers and AI Research Group is focused on exploring the exciting possibilities of quantum computing and its potential impact on the future of technology. Quantum computers are fundamentally different from classical computers—they leverage the principles of quantum mechanics, such as superposition and entanglement, to solve problems that are currently intractable for classical machines. This means quantum computing has the potential to revolutionize industries like cryptography, optimization, and artificial intelligence by providing unprecedented computational power.

Our group is designed for those who are eager to understand and learn about the cutting-edge field of quantum computing. We believe in learning by doing, and this will be a volunteer-based initiative where we explore these concepts together, mentor one another, and grow collectively. Mentorship will be provided by Enis Yazıcı, who has a strong background in physics and engineering, currently teaching at SRH University Heidelberg. For more information or to join the group, please contact Enis Yazıcı at enis.yazici@srh.de.

Areas of Research

  • Quantum Mechanics Basics: Understanding fundamental concepts such as superposition, entanglement, and the uncertainty principle.
  • Quantum Algorithms: Exploring quantum algorithms, including Grover's and Shor's algorithms, and understanding their advantages over classical algorithms.
  • Quantum Computing in AI: Investigating how quantum computing can enhance machine learning and AI, focusing on potential applications and benefits.
  • Quantum Error Correction: Learning about the challenges of quantum computation and the methods used to correct errors in quantum systems.
  • Quantum Cryptography: Exploring quantum encryption methods and understanding how quantum mechanics can make data transmission more secure.

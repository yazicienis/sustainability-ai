Data-Driven Solutions for Water Management in Smart Cities

Exploring smart technologies for sustainable urban water infrastructure

About the Study Group

The Smart Water group develops IoT-enabled monitoring and machine-learning analytics to improve urban water systems: demand forecasting, leak detection, and water-quality insights that support resilient, sustainable cities.

Objectives

  • Integrate distributed IoT sensors for real-time flow/quality monitoring.
  • Apply data analytics & ML for demand forecasts and leak/anomaly detection.
  • Translate evidence into dashboards, alerts, and policy-ready summaries.

Main Areas of Study

  • Smart Water Basics: network topology, metering, uncertainty, QA/QC.
  • IoT & Real-Time Monitoring: sensor selection, low-power nodes, data pipelines.
  • Analytics & ML: time-series, anomaly models, short-term forecasting.
  • Practice & Policy: KPIs, reporting, and governance for sustainable operations.

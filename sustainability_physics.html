About the Study Group

The Physics for a Sustainable Future Study Group is dedicated to understanding the physics behind sustainability. Our primary goal is to provide students with a deep and thorough understanding of fundamental physics concepts, emphasizing how these principles are crucial for developing sustainable solutions. By focusing on the core physics principles, students will gain the skills necessary to make impactful contributions to sustainability.

In this group, we explore a wide range of physics topics, including Chaos Theory, Thermodynamics, and Quantum Physics. We aim to form specialized sub-research groups that allow students to delve deeper into specific areas of interest. This initiative is designed as a long-term, immersive learning journey that encourages students to develop a solid foundation in physics and apply their knowledge to real-world sustainability challenges.

Areas of Study

  • Renewable Energy Systems: Understanding the physics of solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation, focusing on energy efficiency and transformation. Topics include electromagnetic induction, fluid dynamics, and energy conservation.
  • Climate Science: Basics of thermodynamics, the greenhouse effect, and atmospheric physics to understand climate change mechanisms. This includes studying heat transfer, radiation, and the physics of weather systems.
  • Sustainable Materials: Exploring the properties of recyclable and biodegradable materials and their applications in sustainable technologies. Topics include material strength, thermal properties, and conductivity.
  • Water Management: Studying fluid dynamics and its application in water resource management and conservation. Topics include Bernoulli's principle, pressure, and flow rate calculations.
  • Energy Storage: Basics of batteries, capacitors, and innovative storage methods contributing to sustainability. Topics include electrochemistry, electric fields, and energy density.

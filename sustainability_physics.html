About the Research Group

The Physics for a Sustainable Future Research Group is dedicated to understanding the physics behind sustainability. Our aim is to explore the physical principles that contribute to addressing environmental challenges, from renewable energy systems to climate science. By deeply understanding these principles, we can develop innovative solutions that have a real impact.

In this group, we will touch on many diverse areas of physics, ranging from Chaos Theory to Quantum Physics. We plan to form sub-research groups that delve into specific topics, fostering a long-term and immersive learning journey. This initiative is designed to provide a realistic and profound understanding of how fundamental physical laws can drive sustainable technologies and solutions.

Areas of Research

  • Renewable Energy Systems: Understanding the physics of solar, wind, and hydroelectric power generation, focusing on energy efficiency and transformation.
  • Climate Science: Basics of thermodynamics, the greenhouse effect, and atmospheric physics to understand climate change mechanisms.
  • Sustainable Materials: Exploring the properties of recyclable and biodegradable materials and their applications in sustainable technologies.
  • Water Management: Studying fluid dynamics and its application in water resource management and conservation.
  • Energy Storage: Basics of batteries, capacitors, and innovative storage methods contributing to sustainability.

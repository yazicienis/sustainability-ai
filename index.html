Welcome to SustAInability

Our project focuses on leveraging artificial intelligence to improve environmental management and enhance education across cultures. We aim to create sustainable solutions that have a lasting impact on communities across the globe.

The project is a cross-cultural collaboration involving experts and students from Germany, Kyrgyzstan, and Cambodia. By combining diverse perspectives, we are working towards using AI technology to address pressing environmental challenges and contribute to a greener future.

Our partnership includes three leading institutions:

  • SRH University Heidelberg (Germany): A renowned institution committed to providing hands-on education and promoting sustainability.
  • Paragon International University (Cambodia): Focuses on innovation and technological education to foster regional development.
  • University of Central Asia (Kyrgyzstan): Aims to bring higher education to mountainous and rural regions while tackling environmental issues.

Through interactive workshops, study visits, and hands-on project work, we hope to equip students with the skills they need to use AI for environmental sustainability, while also fostering cross-cultural understanding and collaboration.

Our Mission

To foster international collaboration in using AI for sustainability and environmental education. We believe that integrating cutting-edge AI technologies into environmental practices will help address challenges such as climate change, pollution, and resource management.

Get Involved

We are looking for passionate individuals to participate in our workshops, hackathons, and collaborative projects. Whether you are a student, researcher, or someone interested in AI and sustainability, there's a place for you in our mission.

Study Visits and Workshops

Our project includes exciting opportunities such as study visits to Germany and Cambodia, where participants will engage in cultural exchange and learn about the use of AI in sustainability. Workshops cover topics like machine learning, embedded systems, and environmental monitoring.

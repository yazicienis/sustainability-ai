AI and Sustainability Workshop

Extra-curricular workshop for the Master of International Business and Engineering (MIBE)

Overview

The AI and Sustainability workshop brought together MIBE students at SRH Heidelberg to explore how artificial intelligence can be used to design realistic, regulation-ready sustainability solutions for businesses in Germany and the EU. The event was organized within the MIBE startup project ‚ÄúBuild Your Own Business in Germany‚Äù and concluded with a Certificate of Attendance.

The workshop is part of the extra-curricular series in the MIBE program. It does not carry ECTS credits, but it supports the startup project module and prepares students for AI-enabled business practice.

Learning Objectives

Participants worked towards the following goals:

  • Understand typical AI architectures used in sustainability applications (text, vision, time series).
  • Identify free and open-source tools for environmental data, analytics and reporting.
  • Relate AI use cases to EU AI Act, CSRD and ESRS requirements.
  • Design an AI-supported sustainability solution with a clear implementation roadmap.
  • Communicate their concept in a concise, management-oriented pitch.

Program Highlights

1. AI Architectures, Tools and Sustainability Use Cases

Aisha introduced core AI building blocks‚Äîtransformer models for ESG text analysis, computer vision for satellite and sensor images, and forecasting models for energy and resource planning. The session focused on free tools such as Python notebooks, open datasets, and open-source libraries that students can reuse in their MIBE startup projects.

2. EU Regulation and Strategic Design

In the second block, students mapped AI applications to EU AI Act risk levels and CSRD/ESRS reporting needs. They looked at six typical AI roles in sustainability: monitoring, forecasting, process optimisation, supply-chain transparency, compliance reporting, and circular-economy optimisation.

3. Hands-On Group Work

Working in teams of 6‚Äì8, participants selected a concrete sustainability challenge (e.g. energy, mobility, manufacturing, agriculture) and developed an AI-based solution concept. Each team prepared a short implementation plan covering data sources, model choices, business value, risks, and regulatory aspects.

4. How to Talk to AI Agents ‚Äì Effective Prompting

In a compact session led by Prof. Enis Yazici, students learned practical techniques for interacting with AI agents: structuring prompts, defining roles and constraints, iterating with feedback, and turning vague sustainability questions into precise, testable tasks. The focus was on reliable workflows that students can reuse in their own projects and in industry settings.

5. Team Presentations and Feedback

The workshop closed with short pitches in which teams presented their AI-driven sustainability concepts. Feedback centred on innovation, feasibility, user and market understanding, and regulatory awareness‚Äîaligned with the evaluation criteria of the broader MIBE startup project.

Connection to the MIBE Startup Project

The workshop supports the 8-week MIBE startup project by providing an AI layer on top of the existing marketing, entrepreneurship, controlling and research modules. Outcomes can be directly incorporated into:

  • Value Proposition Canvas and Business Model Canvas.
  • Market and user-research sections of the startup concept.
  • Research questions and hypotheses related to AI-enabled solutions.
  • Final pitch presentations and prototype roadmaps.

Participants who completed the workshop received a certificate confirming their competencies in AI-supported sustainability strategy design.

Overview of the Symposium

The symposium “Sustainable Development @ SRH” brought together lecturers, researchers, students, and external partners from different SRH locations to discuss how universities can contribute to climate action, social responsibility, and long-term sustainable development.

The event focused on practical examples from teaching and research, with contributions on climate adaptation, digital transformation, community engagement, and interdisciplinary project formats.

Our Contribution: SustAInability Session

Within this symposium, our international project “SustAInability AI – A Cross-Cultural Partnership for Improving Education and Environmental Management” was represented in a dedicated session on AI-driven environmental management.

The session introduced the SustAInability project as a collaboration between SRH University Heidelberg, the University of Central Asia (Kyrgyzstan), and Paragon International University (Cambodia). The focus lies on using artificial intelligence and data-driven methods to better understand and address local climate and environmental challenges.

The contribution was structured around three main aspects:

  • Project vision and partnerships: Why AI-based approaches are relevant for sustainability and how the project builds a network between Germany, Kyrgyzstan, and Cambodia.
  • Technical prototypes: First concepts and pilot work with IoT sensor systems, environmental data collection, and data analytics for climate-relevant indicators.
  • Educational impact: New teaching formats, student projects, workshops, and study visits that link engineering, AI, and sustainability education.

Presentation by Exchange Student Aisha Toichieva

Aisha Toichieva presenting the SustAInability project at the SRH Symposium.

The session presentation was delivered by Aisha Toichieva, an exchange student from the University of Central Asia, who stayed in Heidelberg for three months within the SustAInability project.

In her talk, Aisha:

  • Introduced the international setup of SustAInability and the motivation for focusing on climate and environmental issues.
  • Explained how AI techniques and IoT-based data acquisition can help to monitor local environmental conditions in Central Asia and Southeast Asia.
  • Described her own work in Heidelberg on environmental sensor data, first analysis workflows, and how these activities are integrated into student learning.
  • Highlighted the importance of cross-cultural exchange, interdisciplinary teamwork, and student mobility for developing responsible technology solutions.

Through this contribution, Aisha represented both the project consortium and the student perspective, showing how international cooperation can directly feed into practical sustainability education.

Key Topics from the SustAInability Project

The presentation and the subsequent discussion at the symposium touched on several core themes of the SustAInability project:

  • AI-supported environmental monitoring: Using machine learning for pattern recognition, anomaly detection, and forecasting in environmental data.
  • Low-cost IoT sensor networks: Designing sensor nodes that are affordable and robust enough for deployment in remote regions.
  • Student-centred project work: Involving students in the full pipeline from sensor installation and data collection to analysis and communication of results.
  • International and intercultural collaboration: Joint workshops, online meetings, and study visits connecting Heidelberg with partner universities in Kyrgyzstan and Cambodia.

These elements make SustAInability both a research-oriented and educational initiative, aligned with our association’s focus on supporting education and dialogue in Heidelberg and Baden-Württemberg.

Relevance for Our Association

As an educational non-profit association based in Heidelberg, we see this contribution to the SRH symposium as an important step in:

  • Strengthening the link between local educational activities and international university partnerships.
  • Giving students the opportunity to present their work in a professional academic setting.
  • Promoting responsible and sustainability-oriented use of digital technologies and AI.

The SustAInability project and Aisha Toichieva’s presentation both support our statutory goals in the fields of education, science, and intercultural dialogue.

Next Steps

Based on this symposium contribution, the project partners plan to:

  • Continue joint student projects and exchange visits related to AI and environmental monitoring.
  • Develop teaching materials and workshops that can also be used in our local educational activities in Heidelberg.
  • Explore further cooperation formats with SRH and other institutions in Baden-Württemberg.

Updates on SustAInability and related activities will be published regularly in the News section of our website.

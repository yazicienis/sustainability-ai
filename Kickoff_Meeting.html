SustAInability — Kickoff Meeting Report

Online • Early 2025 • Cross‑cultural partnership for education and environmental management

Overview

Around 40 participants from SRH Heidelberg, the University of Central Asia, and Paragon International University joined our online kickoff to introduce teams, align expectations, and set up study groups. We reviewed funding context, shared institutional strengths, and discussed how AI, data, and physics‑driven methods can support sustainability goals across partner regions.

BWS — Funding Context

The Baden‑Württemberg Stiftung supports research, education, and sustainable development with an emphasis on innovation and international collaboration. Our project aligns with these goals by promoting practical AI‑powered solutions for environmental management, cross‑cultural teamwork, and student skill‑building.

  • Expectations: interdisciplinary cooperation; hands‑on student training in AI & sustainability
  • Key Outcomes: prototypes & dashboards; stronger SRH–UCA–Paragon partnerships; an e‑book of results
  • Long‑term impact: sustained collaborations and student‑led contributions to SDGs

Study Groups & Mentoring Framework

Groups are formed around complementary skills and interests, mixing partners across countries. Each group has a mentor, a lightweight backlog, and regular checkpoints.

  • Projects overview — group themes and descriptions
  • Mentoring cadence: bi‑weekly check‑ins; milestones tracked per 4–6 weeks
  • Collaboration: Teams channels, shared docs, code repositories, and demo days

Outcomes & Next Steps

  • Introductions completed; communication lines and coordination roles established
  • Initial scoping for: Smart Water, Embedded Systems & IoT, Physics for Sustainability, AI for Climate
  • Action items set for the upcoming AI for Sustainability Workshop and the Heidelberg Summer School 2025
  • Agreement on shared documentation (slides, abstracts) and a public update on the website
